An LLM is a frozen state: trained on massive data and locked in place. The model itself doesn't change. But the products built on top of it (Claude, Copilot) add layers that make it powerful: your prompt gives it context and instructions, tools let it search the web and create files, skills give it specialized know-how, and memory lets it remember your preferences and past conversations over time. The LLM predicts the next word based on patterns. Everything else is scaffolding that makes that prediction remarkably useful.

02
How to Talk to AI
The single most important skill you'll develop.
Think of AI as a brilliant intern with ADHD.

Incredibly capable, but needs clear direction. Vague instructions produce vague results. Be specific about what you want, who you are, and what the output should look like. The quality of the output is a direct reflection of the quality of your input.

Be Specific
Don't say "write me an email." Say "write a follow-up email to a prospect who toured our Chelsea office yesterday, emphasizing the natural light and flexible layout."
Give Context
Tell the AI who you are, who the audience is, and what you're trying to achieve. More context always equals better output.
Define the Output
Want a table? Say so. Want 3 bullet points? Say so. Want a formal tone? Say so. Don't leave format to chance.
Iterate
First draft not right? Don't start over. Tell the AI what to fix. "Make it shorter," "add more urgency," "change the tone." Build on what you have.

Microsoft Copilot for 365
AI built into the tools you already use every day.

Get Copilot Ready

Copilot is built into your Microsoft 365 apps. Complete these steps to get the most out of it.

✓
Open the Copilot app from your taskbar, or press Win + C on your keyboard to launch it instantly
✓
Set up Custom Instructions: click your profile icon (top right) > Settings > Personalization > Edit Instructions. Tell Copilot your name, role, and preferred response format. Learn more →
✓
In the Copilot chat window, look for the Work / Web toggle at the top. Switch to Work to search your org's files, emails, and Teams
Work vs. Web
Work mode searches your org's files, emails, Teams chats, and SharePoint. Web mode searches the public internet. Always check which mode you're in before prompting. Work mode is where Copilot becomes uniquely powerful for your company.
File Search
Stop digging through folders. In Work mode, just ask: "Find the lease agreement for 127 West 26th" or "Show me last quarter's deal pipeline." Copilot searches across SharePoint, OneDrive, Outlook, and Teams.
Personalization
Go to Settings > Personalization > Custom Instructions. Tell Copilot your name, role, and how you like responses formatted. It will apply these preferences to every future conversation. Set it once and forget it. Microsoft's guide →
Instant Access
Copilot is already on your PC. Press the shortcut below from any screen to open it instantly. No browser needed.
Win + C
Copilot in Your Apps

Copilot lives inside Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, and Teams. Look for the Copilot icon in the ribbon or toolbar of each app to open the side panel.

Word

Draft documents from a prompt, rewrite selections to change tone or length, summarize long docs. Agent Mode lets you edit iteratively with Copilot making changes as you guide.

Excel

Analyze data in plain English ("average deal size by neighborhood?"), generate formulas, create charts, highlight trends. No formulas needed.

PowerPoint

Create presentations from a prompt or Word doc. Add slides, reorganize, generate speaker notes. Agent Mode builds polished decks from scratch.

Outlook

Summarize long threads, draft contextual replies, catch up on your inbox hands-free on mobile. Copilot auto-references the email you're viewing.

Teams

Get meeting recaps with decisions and action items, even if you joined late. "What did I miss?" works mid-meeting. Post-call summaries list follow-ups by person.

Agents

Build custom AI assistants with Agent Builder or Copilot Studio. These handle specific tasks from your company's data: answering questions, running workflows, automating processes.

Rethink Your Work with Claude
Not just prompts. New ways to approach what you do every day.

Get Claude Ready

Claude is a separate AI assistant from Anthropic. It excels at long-form writing, analysis, and creative workflows.

✓
Download the desktop app at claude.com/download (Mac or Windows). You can also use it at claude.ai in your browser
✓
Sign in, then click your name (bottom left) > Settings > Profile. Add your name, role, and preferences so Claude tailors every response to you
✓
Enable connectors: go to Settings > Connected Apps to link Google Drive, HubSpot, or Slack. This lets Claude pull your real data into conversations
Space Book Creation
Turn property details into a branded proposal
▾

Upload your property photos and floor plan to Claude, then give it the details. It will generate a full branded proposal you can drop into Canva or send directly.

Sample Prompt
I need you to create a 3-page office space proposal for [ADDRESS], located [CROSS STREETS]. Property details: - [FLOOR INFO], [SQUARE FOOTAGE] - [NUMBER] workstations, [NUMBER] meeting rooms, [AMENITIES] - Monthly asking price: [PRICE] Please create: 1. Cover page with the property address as a bold headline and a 4-image grid layout 2. Floor plan page with a specs panel (address, floor, SF, seating, amenities, description bullets) and the floor plan image 3. Building overview with a marketing description paragraph and 2 exterior photos Use a clean, professional design. I'll provide the images separately.
Tip: Upload all photos at once. Claude will organize them into cover grid, floor plan, and exterior sections.
Lead Generation & Market Signals
Research companies showing office space demand
▾

Use Claude's web research to find companies in your target market that are showing signals they may need office space. Get structured results you can export directly.

Sample Prompt
I'm a commercial real estate broker in [NEIGHBORHOOD/CITY]. Research companies that have shown signals in the past [TIMEFRAME] that they may be looking for office space. Signals to look for: - Recent fundraising rounds - Hiring surges (especially operations, HR, or office-related roles) - New business incorporations in the area - Lease expirations or expansion announcements - Leadership hires suggesting growth Create a spreadsheet with columns: Company Name, Signal Type, Signal Details, Source URL, Estimated Headcount, Potential SF Needed, Contact Info (if available).
Cold Outreach
Personalized broker emails that don't feel templated
▾

Feed Claude the prospect info from your research and let it craft personalized outreach that references specific details about the company.

Sample Prompt
I'm [YOUR NAME], a broker at Nomad Group specializing in [AREA/TYPE] office space in [NEIGHBORHOOD]. Write a personalized cold outreach email to [CONTACT NAME] at [COMPANY NAME]. They recently [SIGNAL: e.g., raised a $12M Series A / expanded their engineering team by 15 people / incorporated in Manhattan]. Reference their specific situation. Keep it under 150 words. Tone: professional but warm, not salesy. End with a soft CTA to connect.
Property & Market Analysis
Comp analysis and neighborhood intelligence
▾

Get Claude to research and compile market data for a property or neighborhood so you can have an informed conversation with clients.

Sample Prompt
Create a market analysis memo for [NEIGHBORHOOD] in [CITY] focused on commercial office space. Include: - Current average asking rents (Class A and Class B) - Vacancy rates and trends over the past 12 months - Notable recent transactions or lease signings - New developments or major renovations in the pipeline - Key demand drivers (industry sectors, transportation, amenities) - How this submarket compares to adjacent neighborhoods Format as a professional memo I can share with a client considering space in this area.
Client Follow-ups
Post-tour recaps and deal communication
▾

After a tour or meeting, use Claude to draft a polished follow-up that captures what was discussed and keeps the deal moving.

Sample Prompt
Draft a follow-up email after a property tour. Client: [NAME] from [COMPANY] Properties toured: [LIST ADDRESSES] Date: [DATE] Key observations during the tour: - [What they liked, concerns, questions they raised] The email should: - Thank them for their time - Recap each property with 1-2 highlights - Note any concerns and how we can address them - Suggest clear next steps (second tour, proposal, landlord meeting) Tone: professional, helpful, not pushy.

HubSpot + Claude

Claude can connect directly to HubSpot to pull deal data, look up contacts, and draft outreach. To enable: open Claude > click your name (bottom left) > Settings > Connected Apps > find HubSpot and authorize access. Once connected, just mention your deals or contacts in any conversation and Claude will pull live data.
